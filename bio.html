About Me

From score to screen — composing music that moves people.

Zhuoran Chu 褚卓燃

I'm a composer and music student at the Eastman School of Music, currently focusing on film scoring and contemporary composition. My journey has been one of continuous exploration — from the structured world of concert music to the narrative-driven realm of film and game audio.

My creative work spans solo pieces, chamber ensembles, film scores, and multimedia projects. I'm deeply interested in how sound shapes perception and emotion, and I'm actively exploring how AI and emerging technologies can expand the possibilities of music production.

My approach blends the traditions of film scoring with influences from jazz and experimental music. I believe that composition and music analysis are deeply interconnected practices — understanding how music works helps us create more effectively.

Game Audio

Game Music

Toymare

A fast-paced puzzle action game where Nox the delivery cat must reassemble toy orders and fend off mischievous toys. I composed the music for the winter chapter — creating atmospheric and dynamic tracks that adapt to exploration, battle, and tense delivery sequences.

The project challenged me to think about interactive music systems and how compositions respond to player actions in real-time.

Concert Works

Chamber & Solo Compositions

Rondos

A concert piece that bridges classical composition techniques with cinematic musical language. The work pays homage to Jerry Goldsmith's Star Trek main title, exploring how film music conventions can translate back into the concert hall.

Ocean-Dreams

An evocative composition inspired by the movement and mystery of the ocean. Blends impressionistic harmonies with modern rhythmic structures.

Radiant Shadow

A reflective solo and chamber work exploring contrasts between light and darkness, both musically and emotionally.
